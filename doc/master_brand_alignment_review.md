# Master Brand Alignment Review (Audit A)

## Corrections applied from R1 (founder direction)

- **Robotics SKU risk — WITHDRAWN.** NuvaTrack R-Series / U-Series / NuvaSpan are treated here as **Cleanuva Robotics commercial product-family definitions**, not claims about current platform software implementation. The Robotics page is evaluated as a **product-family / sales presentation**, which is legitimate brand expression. No "platform-not-implemented" reasoning is used in this review.
- **Future energy / storage — OUT OF SCOPE.** This review keeps the brand framed around **Solar · Platform · Robotics (+ Solutions as the delivery layer)**. Future energy lines are reserved future business and are **not** recommended for the website.
- **AI Copilot → Operational Analyst — phased, not wholesale.** See §"Terminology migration" — recommended as a staged transition (e.g. introduce "Operational Analyst," keep "(AI Copilot)" as a gloss during transition), not a one-shot replacement.

> Scope reminder: this document discusses **brand expression / copy** only. It does not touch navigation, IA, page structure, or technical implementation.

---

## The one-line finding

**On the homepage, Cleanuva currently reads as "an AI software operating layer for solar" — i.e. the brand is collapsed into the Platform.** The master-brand framing (Cleanuva = a clean-energy technology brand whose pillars are Cleanuva Platform, Cleanuva Robotics, and Cleanuva Solutions) is **not surfaced**. This is a copy-level gap, fixable without any structural change.

---

## The 7 questions

### 1. What does a user think Cleanuva is after landing on the homepage?
> "Cleanuva is an **AI-native software operations platform / operating layer for solar** — a smart system you run your plants on."

Driven by: hero eyebrow *"The AI-native operating layer for solar,"* headline *"It unifies the fragments / coordinates the work / runs the operation,"* and subline *"…the system you run solar on, not another dashboard."* Every top-of-page brand cue points at **software**. Robotics, proof, and execution are perceived as *features of that software*, not peer pillars of a broader brand.

### 2. Will they mistake Cleanuva = Platform?
**Yes — high risk.** This is the dominant misread. The brand name, eyebrow, headline, and subline all equate "Cleanuva" with "the operating layer / the system / the software." A first-time visitor has no cue that **Cleanuva is broader than the Platform**. (Master Brief explicitly flags "Cleanuva = Cleanuva Platform → No.")

### 3. Will they mistake Cleanuva = Robotics?
**No — low risk** (the opposite problem). Robotics is clearly sub-positioned as "one connected execution layer." If anything it is **under-expressed as a peer business pillar** — a visitor sees robots as a capability *inside* the software story, not as the co-equal **Cleanuva Robotics** product line the brief intends.

### 4. How to strengthen the master brand WITHOUT changing nav/structure?
Pure copy moves:
- **Open with the brand, not the software.** Add a brand sentence near the hero/eyebrow: *"Cleanuva is a clean-energy technology brand for solar — AI software, robotics, and proven results."* (Solar-scoped per R1.)
- **Name the three pillars as proper nouns** wherever they appear: **Cleanuva Platform** (the AI software), **Cleanuva Robotics** (the first-party robots + flagship execution adapter), **Cleanuva Solutions** (packaged delivery). Today copy says "the platform" (lowercase, generic) — capitalizing and naming the lines makes the architecture legible.
- **Reframe the operating-layer language as the Platform line**, not the brand: "Cleanuva **Platform** is the AI-native operating layer…" so "operating layer" describes one pillar, not all of Cleanuva.
- **Elevate Robotics to peer-pillar voice**: "Cleanuva Robotics" as a named product line, not "one execution layer."
- **Add a brand-architecture micro-line** in the footer/closing: *"Cleanuva Platform · Cleanuva Robotics · Cleanuva Solutions — one clean-energy brand."*
- **Name Solutions as the delivery layer** in copy: "Cleanuva Solutions packages platform + robotics + integration into customer solutions."

All of the above are sentence/label edits — **no nav, no structure, no new pages.**

### 5. Which pages most need brand-layer expression added?
1. **Homepage** — highest leverage; the hero/opening sets the whole brand read (currently = software).
2. **Company** — vision pillars currently read Platform / AI Copilot / Robotics; should read **Cleanuva Platform / Robotics / Solutions**, and the "Building the operating layer" lead should become master-brand language.
3. **Robotics** — should lead as **"Cleanuva Robotics," a named product line / pillar**, not just "NuvaTrack robots connected to the platform."
4. **Platform** — small but important: clarify it is **"Cleanuva Platform," one product line**, so "operating layer" stops standing in for the whole brand.

### 6. Which pages already express it (closer to) correctly?
- **Company page** is the closest: it already states *"Cleanuva is a brand of NETRO Sparkle GmbH,"* has a three-pillar vision block, and an entity/legal seam — the scaffolding is right; only the pillar labels and lead need brand-aligning.
- **Footer / Legal** correctly keep **NETRO Sparkle GmbH** as the legal entity (not product chrome) and carry the "Cleanuva is a brand of…" seam. ✓
- **Robotics product naming** (NuvaTrack/NuvaSpan as a family) is acceptable product-family expression (per R1) — it just isn't yet wrapped in the **"Cleanuva Robotics"** pillar voice.

### 7. Which changes are minimal-effort, maximum-impact?
| Move | Effort | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Add a master-brand sentence at the homepage hero/opening | 1 sentence | **Very high** — fixes the #1 misread |
| Name the three pillars as proper nouns across existing copy ("Cleanuva Platform/Robotics/Solutions") | find-and-name pass | **High** — makes the architecture legible everywhere |
| Company vision pillars → Platform / Robotics / Solutions | 3 labels + descs | **High** |
| Reframe "operating layer" as "Cleanuva Platform" (one pillar) | a few words | **High** — stops brand=software collapse |
| Footer brand-architecture micro-line | 1 line | **Medium-high** — reinforces brand on every page |
| Lead Robotics with "Cleanuva Robotics" pillar voice | 1–2 lines | **Medium** |

## Prioritized recommendations (brand expression only)

**P0 — fix the brand=software collapse**
- **R1.** Homepage opening: add a master-brand sentence so the first brand cue is *"Cleanuva is a clean-energy technology brand (AI software + robotics + proven results)"* — not "the operating layer." *(Hero eyebrow/subline / opening copy.)*
- **R2.** Reframe "operating layer / operating system" language as **Cleanuva Platform** (one product line), not the whole brand. *(Hero, Platform hero, OperatingModel, Company.)*

**P1 — make the three pillars legible**
- **R3.** Name **Cleanuva Platform / Cleanuva Robotics / Cleanuva Solutions** as proper nouns consistently across existing copy. *(Homepage sections, Platform, Robotics, Company, Footer.)*
- **R4.** Company vision pillars → **Platform / Robotics / Solutions** (replace the "AI Copilot" middle pillar with the Solutions pillar; keep the analyst concept inside the Platform pillar). *(Company.vision.)*
- **R5.** Elevate Robotics to **peer-pillar** voice — "Cleanuva Robotics, our first-party robot line and the platform's flagship execution adapter." *(Robotics overview, ConnectedExecution, Company.)*

**P2 — reinforce**
- **R6.** Footer/closing brand-architecture micro-line ("Cleanuva Platform · Robotics · Solutions — one clean-energy brand"). *(Footer / FinalCta.)*
- **R7.** Name **Cleanuva Solutions** as the delivery/packaging layer in copy (without changing the Solutions page's role content). *(Solutions intro, Company.)*
- **R8.** Begin the **Operational Analyst** terminology transition (see below) as part of brand voice.

## Terminology migration recommendation (AI Copilot → Operational Analyst)

**Do not do a one-shot global replace.** Recommended **phased** path:

- **Phase 1 (now):** introduce **"Operational Analyst"** as the canonical name on the most visible surfaces (homepage + Platform Copilot section, section eyebrow/title), with a transitional gloss **"Operational Analyst (formerly AI Copilot)"** *or* simply lead with "Operational Analyst" while leaving secondary mentions for Phase 2. Keep the *concept* (grounded, source-cited, human-in-the-loop) unchanged.
- **Phase 2:** sweep remaining "AI Copilot" mentions in nav/footer labels, the diagnosis card, and the Company pillar; drop the gloss once "Operational Analyst" is established.
- **Phase 3:** rename the internal `Copilot.*` namespace / `ai-copilot.tsx` / `copilot.ts` for code hygiene (no user-visible change by then).

Rationale: "AI Copilot" is pervasive (~20 strings/locale + components + labels) and was the centerpiece of the V4.1 content work; a staged transition protects brand continuity, avoids a 4-locale scramble, and lets you validate the "Operational Analyst" name before committing everywhere. *(This is a recommendation; no change made.)*
