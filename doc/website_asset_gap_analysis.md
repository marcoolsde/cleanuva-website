# Cleanuva Website — Asset Gap Analysis

## 0. The core gap

The site has a **strong design system and a coherent V4.1 narrative**, but almost every visual is **synthetic or placeholder**: CSS/SVG abstractions, gradient "scene" plates, stylized HTML mockups. That is exactly what reads as *"well-designed concept"* rather than *"a company with a real product you can buy."* Closing the gap is **90% an asset problem, not a design problem.**

**Two buckets (this is the key distinction):**

- **Producible now** — real assets you can create without a customer: product UI screenshots/prototype, a Copilot demo video, robot photography/renders, product visuals, hero imagery, company/founder/team photos. These remove most of the "concept" feel.
- **Earned, not produced** — assets that require a real pilot/customer and their consent, and that the trust-and-claims policy forbids fabricating: case studies, customer logos, testimonials, real-deployment photography, third-party certifications. These deliver the *final* credibility leap and cannot be shortcut.

A blunt truth: several of the highest-value visuals (Platform screenshots, Copilot demo) **depend on the product UI actually existing** (the V4.1 build / `app.cleanuva.ai`). Asset progress is partly gated on product progress.

---

## 1. Homepage imagery

- **Current:** Synthetic cinematic hero (CSS backdrop + SVG perspective field + abstract data layer + small robot). All section imagery is abstract (Fragmentation, OperatingModel). No real photography or product shot. (The "Intelligence Over the Field" art-direction brief exists but was never produced — deferred Step 12.)
- **Required real assets:** one decisive hero visual — **either** authentic utility-scale / C&I rooftop solar photography (operator-credible, not stock), **or** a high-fidelity **product-UI hero** (Copilot / Command Center) consistent with V4.1's Copilot-primary positioning, **or** a composite (field + UI overlay). Plus 1–2 supporting section images.
- **Why it matters:** the hero is the first credibility signal; a synthetic hero says "concept."
- **Bucket / Priority:** Producible now · **High** · *Decision required:* field photography vs product-UI hero (resolve direction before commissioning).

## 2. Platform screenshots

- **Current:** The Command Center is a **stylized HTML/CSS mockup**; IntelArc is an SVG; no real product screenshots anywhere. The Platform page sells a SaaS platform with **zero real UI**.
- **Required real assets:** genuine product screenshots (or high-fidelity, clearly-real prototype UI) of — **(a)** the Command Center dashboard, **(b)** the AI Copilot conversation, **(c)** a CSV / inspection-PDF analysis output, **(d)** a verified-recovery / VERIFY report. These map 1:1 to the page's claims.
- **Why it matters:** **the single biggest credibility gap.** A product company's platform page without real screenshots reads as vaporware to B/D/E financial-grade buyers.
- **Bucket / Priority:** Producible now **but gated on the product UI existing** · **Critical**. Must be real product or honestly-labeled product mockups (no faked dashboards passed as live).

## 3. Robotics photography

- **Current:** `PhotoPlate` gradient/"scene" placeholders (golden-hour washes), **no real robot imagery**.
- **Required real assets:** real photography **or** photorealistic renders of NuvaTrack R-Series, U-Series, and NuvaSpan — hero shot, on-array/in-operation shot, scale/with-human shot, and detail/spec shots. As robotics is partner/OEM-enabled, OEM/partner photography (with usage rights) or commissioned renders are both viable.
- **Why it matters:** robotics is a **product you sell today**; gradient placeholders undercut "buy this now" and the Tesla/Apple product framing.
- **Bucket / Priority:** Producible now · **High**. Must be the actual hardware (or accurate renders) — never stock robots.

## 4. Product-page visuals

- **Current:** Same placeholder plates + spec tables; the Compare page is text/table only.
- **Required real assets:** per-model hero render/photo, configuration & feature imagery, a dimensions/scale diagram, an autonomy/operation visualization, and comparison visuals for the Compare page.
- **Why it matters:** Tesla/Apple-style product pages convert on **real product visuals**; specs alone don't.
- **Bucket / Priority:** Producible now · **High** · depends on #3 (robotics photography/renders).

## 5. Videos / animations

- **Current:** framer-motion micro-interactions, count-ups, CSS animations. **No real video.**
- **Required real assets, in priority order:**
  1. **A short Copilot demo video** dramatizing the two V4.1 scenarios — *"which plants should I service this week?"* → verified recovery, and *"drop a PDF"* → tracked dispatches. **The most persuasive single asset for the platform.**
  2. **Robot-in-operation video** (autonomous cleaning on-array) — proof the hardware is real and works.
  3. Optional 20–40s brand/hero loop.
- **Why it matters:** for a software+hardware company, a real demo video is the strongest conversion and "this is real" asset.
- **Bucket / Priority:** Producible now (demo gated on product UI; robot video gated on a filmable unit) · **High**.

## 6. Trust assets

- **Current:** Text-only TrustStrip / TrustSection (honest early-stage signals). No logos, no certifications, no metrics, no people.
- **Required real assets — producible now (no customer needed):** real founder/team photos, a genuine company/Germany presence shot, partner/OEM logos **as partnerships are signed (with rights)**, security/compliance attestations **once actually obtained**, any real events/press.
- **Required real assets — earned (gated):** customer logos, testimonials.
- **Why it matters:** a real face + real company presence convert "anonymous concept" into "real team you can trust," without violating the no-fabrication policy.
- **Bucket / Priority:** Mixed · **Medium-High** for the producible-now subset (founder/team/company); the rest follow real relationships.

## 7. Case-study assets

- **Current:** None. The Outcome example is explicitly "illustrative"; Resources are "coming soon."
- **Required real assets:** at least **one real, permission-cleared pilot/case study** with real numbers — recovered yield, verified outcome, before/after — plus (ideally) the customer's logo and a quote.
- **Why it matters:** **the asset that most separates "concept" from "credible product company."** Financial-grade buyers (B/D/E) and renewal-driven O&M (C) buy on proof.
- **Bucket / Priority:** **Earned, not produced** · **Critical-but-gated.** Requires a real pilot/customer + consent + real data. Cannot be fabricated (trust-and-claims policy). This is the one gap you cannot shortcut — it must be *won*.

## 8. Priority-ranked asset acquisition roadmap

| Tier | Assets | Bucket | Depends on |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0 — Highest leverage** | Real **Platform product screenshots** (Command Center, Copilot, CSV/PDF analysis, VERIFY report) + the **Copilot demo video** | Producible now | The product UI / prototype existing |
| **1 — Sell-today products** | **Robotics photography/renders** (#3) + **product-page visuals** (#4) + **robot-in-operation video** | Producible now | A real/render-ready unit |
| **2 — First impression** | **Homepage hero** real imagery (field vs product-UI — decide first) | Producible now | Direction decision |
| **3 — Human credibility** | **Founder/team/company photos**; **partner/OEM logos** as signed | Producible now / as relationships land | — |
| **4 — The credibility leap (earned)** | **First pilot case study** + **customer logos** + **testimonials** + **real-deployment photography** + **certifications** | Earned, gated | A real customer + consent + real data |

## 9. Constraints & honesty guardrails

- **No fabrication** (trust-and-claims policy): no invented customers, logos, testimonials, certifications, deployments, or case studies. Tier-4 assets are *earned*.
- **Real or clearly-labeled.** Product screenshots must be real product or honestly-labeled mockups; robot imagery must be the actual hardware or accurate renders — never stock.
- **Product-gated assets:** Platform screenshots and the Copilot demo depend on the V4.1 product UI existing; sequence them with product progress.
- **One decision blocks Tier 2:** the homepage hero direction (authentic field photography vs product-UI hero) should be settled before commissioning.

**Bottom line:** Tiers 0–3 are *producible now* and would move the site decisively from "concept" toward "credible product company"; **Tier 4 (a real pilot case study + first logos) is the final, un-shortcuttable leap** and depends on winning the first reference customer — not on more website work.
